About Grapefruit

Grapefruit, (Citrus paradisi), citrus tree of the Rutaceae family and its edible fruit. The grapefruit probably originated in Barbados as a hybrid of shaddock (Citrus grandis). It became well established as a fruit for home consumption in the islands of the West Indies before its culture spread to the American mainland.

History

Grapefruit has become popular as breakfast fruit in various parts of the world, and production has expanded to most citrus-growing countries, notably the United States, Israel, Cyprus, South Africa, and Brazil. As a source of vitamin C, the grapefruit is exceeded among common fruits only by the orange and lemon. of eating grapefruit include supporting immunity and controlling blood sugar. Grapefruit contains vitamin C, as well as water and fiber, which can improve your digestion.
